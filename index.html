Sambucus - family Adoxaceae

Sambucus is commonly known as Elderberry. The juice of its deep bluish-purple berries have the interesting property of turning red when mixed with water... which is pretty magical. Your imaginative nature and love for wisdom makes the Elderberry plant a good match for you.

Nymphaea Candida

Also called a Dwarf White Water Lily, Nymphaea Candida features small, creamy white blossoms with a lemon yellow center. It lives only in still water, and usually floats alone. It's independent, yet goes-with-the-flow as needed, which is something you and Nymphaea Candida might have in common.

Lolium

Ambitious and dynamic like yourself, this plant can adapt to challenging situations, sometimes so well that it can overwhelm other plants. Lolium is a genus of grasses in the bluegrass family, sometimes called Ryegrass (not to be confused with rye!). These grasses are often used for pastures, grazing fields, lawns, and can be found virtually everywhere.

Buxus (Boxwood)

This plant features typically small emerald green leaves, & a very fine wood grain. It has been used for intricate wood carving and making musical instruments since the days of Heracles. It was also a very popular wood used to carve wood blocks for block printing. Your appreciation for music and creativity makes this a great spirit plant for you!

Vitis Vinifera - Common Grapevine

This plant has somewhere between 5,000 and 10,000 varieties, and has been cultivated for thousands of years... especially to make wine. To say that it's a versatile plant with a long history in human agriculture and history is an understatement. Your interest in many pursuits and appreciation for passion makes the grapevine a good match for you.

Blue Pimpernel (formerly Angallis Foemina)

This plant is very small, but very amazing. It is also known as Poor Man's Weatherglass, its striking blue flowers close as bad weather is approaching, and open up when the sun is out. Blue pimpernel may appear spiny and weedy at first, but it is actually a very beautiful, gentle plant. Its responsiveness to its environment, make it a great match for your intuitive nature.

Mandragora (Mandrake)

The Mandrake has pastel violet flowers, broad, pointy leaves, and once dug up, a root that resembles two legs dancing. The plant itself is poisonous, and its root has hallucinogenic and narcotic properties. It said to have been used in witchcraft, and has been treated with superstition throughout history. This plant's appearance is a good match for your vivacious nature, as it, too, appears to be full-of-life!

Common Juniper (Juniperus Communis)

Slow-growing Juniper can vary a lot in size. It can be found as a low, sprawling shrub, or as trees up to 10 meters tall. Known for its pleasant aroma, the "berries" (which are actually seed cones) can be used to flavor ales, beers, gin, and some foods. Juniper is a good match for your practical and diligent nature, as it makes the most out of its environment, even if it takes a while.

Rockcap Fern (Polypodium)

Rockcap Ferns come in many varieties. You can find them growing either on the ground or as epiphytes, attached to the surfaces of other plants. Polypodium gets part of its name from the Ancient Greek word for "little foot", calling to the foot-like appearance of the rhizome. Your appreciation for balance and harmony is reflected well by symmetry of these ferns, and their appreciation for serene, temperate environments.

Acanthus

The broad leaves of the Acanthus plant, found in the Mediterranean Basin and in Asia, have a spiny, layered appearance. Acanthus leaf motifs have been widely used in architectural decoration throughout history, from Corinthian to modern times. Fittingly, the Acanthus has long been used as a symbol of long life and immortality. Your strength and steadfastness make the Acanthus a good match for you!

Arum Lilies

Arum lilies (from the ancient greek "Aron"), are not actually related to true lilies. They are a very showy plants, and always have a distinctive hooded leaf resembling a flower. Plants in the arum family are very diverse and include plants such as the Snake lily, the Cuckoo-Pint, and the Aronstab. Your love of adventure and tendency to leave the beaten bath is exemplified by the distinctively arrow-shaped leaves of the Arum!

Water Plantain

Alisma plantago-aquatica, also known as the common water plantain, is an aquatic plant whose blossoms open in the afternoon. The word "alisma" is Celtic in origin, and it means "shallow water". This plant often grows on muddy land that is frequently flooded with water. Your serious and independent nature, coupled with your ability to appreciate things both material and emotional make the Alisma a good fit for you!
